Story

I had just gotten a new car and was driving along when I heard a beep warning me that I was running low on fuel. I thought this was the absolute best thing in the world, the car has automated checking when I'm low on fuel for me!

I then started thinking wait.. I'm a software developer. I didn't feel that I should be so impressed by something so simple. At this point it dawned on me that I had spent a career creating tools to help businesses or other people, but never created anything for my own benefit. That seemed like a pretty important perspective to get and so this project was born!

I was going through physio for a bad back injury at the time and I knew that I had a bad habit of pushing myself hard instead of being consistent, which would lead to be burning out. This app was going to be awesome and help me break that bad cycle!

In agile fashion, I started with the simplest thing that would be useful. At first, this was my whiteboard:

I had great ambitions about all the great things that this phone app would do for me. I can't even remember what they were now but I do remember that in the end, the most fantastic thing was that the app acted like a calculator and added up my number of reps so that I didn't have to.

The app slowly evolved and I learned a lot about mobile development and gained a lot of perspective about what features were helpful.
